## Who Built This and Why

My name is Ahmad. I am a university student and I live in Al Quaa.

I did not read about this problem in a research paper. I see it every day.

The farmers around me — my neighbours, my community — spend real time every week trying to find basic things they need. A water tanker for the farm. A generator for an event. Someone with a pickup truck to move equipment. The way it works today is simple: you call people you know, they call people they know, and eventually maybe you find what you need. If you are new here, or if the person you need to call is busy, you are stuck.

I have watched farmers waste hours on the phone for something that should take five minutes. I have seen people pay more than they should because they had no way to compare. I have seen equipment sitting unused on one farm while someone two kilometres away is desperately looking for it.

I am a university student. I know how to build things. And I live here. So I built this.

**Mawared Al Quaa is not a startup idea. It is a tool I want my neighbours to actually use.**

The app is completely free. It will always be free. Every dirham exchanged goes directly from the person who needs something to the person who provides it. The app takes nothing. No fees, no commission, no cut — ever. It is a community service, not a business.

## The Problem

Al Quaa is a dispersed rural community in Al Ain where residents, farmers, and small business owners regularly need shared resources: water tankers, generators, 4×4 vehicles, farm tools, event tents, temporary workers, and cold transport.

Today this exchange happens entirely through personal contacts and phone calls.

> **If you don't know the right person, you waste time, overpay, or go without.**

The problem is not that resources don't exist — they do. The problem is that they are **invisible**. A farmer 4 km away may have an idle generator available tonight. A household may have a water tanker free this afternoon. Nobody knows.

This is a **daily** friction affecting every farm owner, every household, and every small entrepreneur in Al Quaa.

## The Solution

**Mawared Al Quaa** (موارد القاع) makes hidden local resources visible and accessible.

A farmer who needs a generator tonight opens the app, posts the request, and within minutes sees three neighbours who have one available — with their distance, price, and rating. He taps WhatsApp and talks directly to the person. Done. No phone tree. No wasted hour. No middleman.

A resident who has a pickup truck sitting idle in the evenings lists it on the app. People who need transport see it. He earns money from something he already owns. The community benefits from a resource that was invisible before.

### Core features

| Feature | What it does |
|---------|-------------|
| 🔴 **Request** | Post what you need — type, timing, budget, delivery preference |
| 🟢 **Offer** | List what you have — equipment, vehicles, tools, skills |
| 🔵 **Browse** | Search and filter available resources near you |
| 🟡 **Urgent today** | See and respond to same-day requests that need an answer now |
| 💬 **WhatsApp connect** | One tap opens WhatsApp with a pre-written message in Arabic or English — talk directly, no middleman |

### Two features designed specifically for this community

**1. Trust circles**

Every listing can be visible to:
- 👪 Family and neighbours only
- ✅ Verified Al Quaa residents
- 🌐 Public

This is not a generic feature. This is how Al Quaa actually works. People here already share and lend — but only with people they trust. The app does not ask them to change that. It just makes it easier.

**2. Micro-business suggestions**

When a resident lists what they own, the app suggests a small business they could build from it:

> *You have a pickup truck + free evenings → Evening local delivery service*
> *You have a generator → Rent it to events and farms when you're not using it*

A farmer in Al Quaa may not think of himself as an entrepreneur. This feature shows him that what he already owns could be the start of something.

## 💚 100% Free — Always

Mawared Al Quaa is a community service. Not a business. Not a startup.

- **Free for everyone** — no subscription, no listing fee, no premium tier, no ads
- **Zero commission** — when a farmer pays 150 AED for a generator, all 150 AED goes to the person who owns the generator. The app takes nothing.
- **Direct contact only** — every deal is made person to person via WhatsApp. The app is just the introduction.
- **The app is the tool. The community owns the value.**

I am a student from Al Quaa. I am not trying to make money from my neighbours. I built this because it should exist.

Sustainability comes from potential future partnership with Al Ain Municipality or local community sponsors — never from charging the people who use it.

## Feasibility

### Why this works in Al Quaa specifically

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| 📱 **Device access** | Smartphones are universal in Al Quaa. The app runs in any mobile browser — no download, no app store |
| 📶 **Connectivity** | Mobile data covers Al Quaa. Core features need minimal data |
| 💬 **Contact method** | All contact goes through WhatsApp — every resident already uses it daily |
| 🌐 **Language** | Arabic-first, full RTL layout, instant toggle to English |
| 🔐 **Onboarding** | Phone number only, SMS verified. No email, no password complexity |
| 💵 **Cost to run** | Single HTML file — free hosting on GitHub Pages or Netlify |

### What it does NOT require
- ❌ App store approval
- ❌ Proprietary hardware
- ❌ AI costs
- ❌ Complex backend at launch
- ❌ Any technical knowledge from residents to use it

### Deployment path

```
Month 1   →  Deploy to a public URL
              Share with 20–30 Al Quaa residents via WhatsApp
              Collect first real listings

Month 2–3 →  Add SMS verification (Unifonic UAE, ~0.05 AED per SMS)
              Add a live database (Supabase free tier)
              First real connections happening

Month 6+  →  Partnership with Al Ain Municipality as a funded community service
              Expand to similar UAE rural communities
```
